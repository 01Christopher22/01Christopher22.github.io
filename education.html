Graduate

Michigan State University

I attended Michigan State University from 2011 to 2016. During my first year I was a graduate teaching assistant in the chemistry department performing recitation sessions for introductory chemistry. In my second year I became a graduate research assistant. My research was focused on the nuclear structure, in particular nuclear shape coexistence, of neutron-rich nuclei around the N=40 neutron sub-shell closure. During my tenure as a graduate student I spent three years on the electronics committee and one year as graduate student president. I also participated in several outreach events spreading knowledge and excitement of nuclear science to members of the public of all ages. In fall of 2016 I defended my thesis and earned a Ph.D. in nuclear chemistry.

Degrees and Coursework
2016: Ph.D. Nuclear Chemistry GPA: 3.72/4.0

Undergraduate

Minnesota State University, Mankato

I attended Minnesota State University, Mankato from 2005 to 2011. My first year was as a senior in high school as a post-secondary student. After graduating high school I enrolled at MSU, Mankato and declared Physics as my major with the intent of also completing the pre-medicine curriculum. After the third year I declared Chemistry as a second major and Mathematics as a minor. I also began performing research in low-energy nuclear physics, which ultimately changed my career path from pursuing a career in medicine to a career nuclear physics. In 2010 I attended the DOE Nuclear Chemistry Summer School and graduated in 2011 with a BS degree in both physics and chemistry with a minor in mathematics.

Degrees and Coursework
2011: B.S. Physics and Chemistry (Magna Cum Laude) GPA: 3.67/4.0
2010: DOE Nuclear Chemistry Summer School GPA: 3.96/4.0
