Welcome to My Website

Introduction and About me

My name is Christopher Prokop. Professionally, I am a Director's Postdoctoral Fellow at Los Alamos National Laboratory. My research is focused on low-energy nuclear physics, in particular measurement of neutron-capture and neutron-induced charged-particle reaction cross sections. I am also involved in a variety of mission-enabling activities such as data acquisition and analysis software development, high-performance computing, and detector design and simulation. Personally, I have a wide variety of interests and hobbies. As you may have gathered from the two photos you've seen thus far I am a car guy and I enjoy the outdoors. Additionally I enjoy carpentry, weightlifting, shooting any variety of firearm, video and board games, and I also dabble a bit with playing guitar. Presently the purpose of this website is primarily to learn HTML, CSS, bootstrap, etc.

Education

I earned my Ph.D. in Nuclear Chemistry from Michigan State University in 2016. Prior to that I received a B.S. in Physics and in Chemistry with a minor in Mathematics from Minnesota State University, Mankato in 2011. I also completed the DOE Nuclear Chemistry Summer School at San Jose State University in 2010.
