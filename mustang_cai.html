Stock Interior

On the 2016 Mustang GT Premium the default dash vent panel color is matte silver and there are 3 circular vents above the infotainment system. While the matte silver is not a bad asthetic choice I changed this to black. I also replaced the central vent with the LUND Racing nGauge®. The nGauge® provides an interface through which an aftermarket tune can be loaded into the vehicle. Additionally it displays and records a series of real-time diagnostics essential for evaluating vehicle performance.


PlastiDip® of the Dash Vent Panel

After removing the four matte silver pieces comprising the dash vent panels I removed the vents and layed the panels on top of newspaper on my work bench. I proceeded to apply black PlastiDip® in several very light and even coats. The total number of coats was 12. PlastiDip® is a rubberized coating that is applied much like spray paint but if applied in a thick enough layer can be simply peeled away from most surfaces if no longer desired. For this application I wanted each coat to be incredibly light such that it wouldnt gloss out and the final product would have a smooth textured matte black appearance to match the housing around the infotainment system.


Finished Interior

The next day, after allowing 15 hours for the refinished dash panels to dry, I routed the cable for connecting the nGauge® to OBD diagnostics port and reinstalled the panels into the vehicle. I then installed the nGauge® vent mount, which fits perfectly in the opening for the center vent. Finally I plugged in the nGauge, inserted a micro SD card loaded with the new tune, most recent LUND Racing nGauge® firmware, and snapped the gauge into its mount. I feel the black dash panels look much cleaner and complete a more agressive look for the interior of the Mustang. With the interior modifications made I turned my attention to under the hood.


Under the Hood

Under the hood the work was very simple and took only 10 minutes. The JLT intake comes with simple and complete installation instructions. After installing the JLT intake, I returned to the inside of the car and flashed the new tune to the ECM. (Since the JLT intake diameter is different than the stock intake an aftermarket tune is required). After another few minutes I started the car and took it for a drive. The first thing I noticed was an increase in power along with dramatic improvments in throttle response and agressiveness of the transmission shifting gears. Essentially "Normal" mode now feels like "Sport +" mode and I am very pleased with the results. I strongly suggest this be the first performance upgrade one should do to their mustang.
